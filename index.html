About Me

Originally from New Jersey, I moved to Texas in 2016. My father was born here, so I have a lot of family in the area. Being the aunt of 7 kids (and counting!) being around for family events has been a blessing. Coming from an area with a cold, dark winter that lasts too long, I prefer the heat by a vast majority. "Up north, you spend winter inside because it's too cold to go out. In the south, you spend summer inside because it's too hot to go out. The difference is you don't have to shovel sunshine." - My (very wise) mother.

For 10 years, I have worked as a Graphic Designer for two different companies. Both focused more on technical, standards-driven design rather than the creative, client-driven design found in most Graphic Design jobs. Unfortunately, this translates to most Graphic Design jobs who don't like my portfolio. I've always liked coding. I wrote programs on my TI-83 calculator in high school, used HTML to make my myspace page include gifs and pictures in the main section, and my favorite design class was Flash. I even created my original website completely in Flash because I loved the coding. Of course, Flash is no longer used at all and has been phased out of everything.

My transition from Graphic Design to Web Development was an inevitability. I am the only Graphic Designer I know who can't draw. I can't even draw stick figures with equal length arms! But I can make an amazing safety label or city wayfinding sign. Or Photoshop all of your old pictures so they look as good as the day they were printed. Coding takes me back to when outside education was a fun distraction from boring schoolwork. When I looked forward to finding out how to use pure text to produce a result that looked great and was interactive. When I used that knowledge to make something specific to me because that's what I was interested in at the time.

I look forward to being able to combine my two fields of study to create new and better things. The average person works for over 40 years. My first decade was Graphic Design. My next three will take me back to the wonderment of youth. And I don't think I'd want it any other way.
